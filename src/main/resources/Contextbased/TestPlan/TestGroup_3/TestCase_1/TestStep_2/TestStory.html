Description

At 8:00 AM the attending physician determines that the patient is suffering from carbon monoxide poisoning. She orders a hospital admission for the patient to receive hyperbaric oxygen therapy (HBOT), overnight observation, and a full neurological assessment. At 8:10 AM, the physician updates the patient's record with a working diagnosis, coded with the SNOMED CT code 17383000 (Toxic effect of carbon monoxide (disorder)), and orders admission for carbon monoxide poisoning. Â Southern Midwest Medical Center reports syndromic surveillance data to the city health department (CHD). At 8:15 AM on February 1, 2010, the hospital's electronic health record module for syndromic surveillance data assembles and transmits an Update message about this encounter to SHD.

Comments

This Test Case provides an example of an ED visit for which the patient's chief complaint is captured as free-text, working diagnosis and final diagnosis are captured with SNOMED CT codes, the patient is discharged from the ED and admitted for inpatient care, and the Admit/Encounter Reason is captured with a SNOMED CT code. Dates and times are provided in this test case to illustrate the sequence of clinical and messaging events. Since the exact dates and times are not reproducible when modeling the Test Case with a Health IT Module, only date and time format will be validated within tester submitted test data.
